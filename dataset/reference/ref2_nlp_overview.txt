Natural language processing (NLP) is a field of computer science and artificial intelligence concerned with the interactions between computers and human language. It involves the application of computational techniques to the analysis and synthesis of natural language and speech. NLP tasks include tokenisation, part-of-speech tagging, named entity recognition, parsing, sentiment analysis, machine translation, and question answering. Modern NLP systems rely heavily on deep learning architectures such as transformers. The transformer model, introduced in the paper "Attention Is All You Need", uses self-attention mechanisms to process sequences in parallel, allowing it to capture long-range dependencies efficiently. Pre-trained language models like BERT, GPT, and T5 have revolutionised NLP by achieving remarkable performance across a wide range of benchmarks. Fine-tuning these models on domain-specific data further improves their accuracy for specialised tasks such as medical text analysis, legal document review, and code generation. Text preprocessing steps such as lowercasing, stopword removal, stemming, and lemmatisation are fundamental to preparing raw text for NLP pipelines.
